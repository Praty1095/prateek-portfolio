About Me

From Engineering Foundations to Procurement Strategy

My journey began in mechanical engineering, which helped me understand how materials, processes, and cost efficiency fit together. Over time, I moved into procurement, where I could directly influence how businesses manage suppliers, spending, and operational flow.

Over the past six years, I’ve managed end-to-end procurement for both manufacturing and IT service companies. My work has included sourcing strategy, supplier evaluation, and contract negotiation, all focused on improving cost efficiency, compliance, and supplier performance.

Recently, I completed my MBA at Loughborough University, where I explored how AI-driven contract management systems can transform procurement operations. Today, I focus on building procurement processes that are simple, transparent, and scalable, aligning business goals with measurable outcomes.

Case Studies

Real projects that demonstrate how I approach challenges and deliver results.

Challenge:The company relied on manual paperwork and physical approvals for supplier contracts, leading to delays and version control issues. Each renewal could take up to six weeks, and missing signatures sometimes caused project holdups.
Action:I identified this bottleneck and proposed digitising the contract approval process. I coordinated with internal IT teams to integrate an e-signature workflow and collaborated with suppliers to ensure legal and operational compliance. I started the rollout with a few suppliers who already had digital infrastructure, then expanded adoption across all major vendors.
Result:Contract approval time reduced by 70% (from 6 weeks to less than 2). Administrative errors dropped significantly due to version tracking. Improved cross-department collaboration, as approvals became traceable and secure.

Challenge:Vivo faced significant supply disruptions during COVID-19 due to dependence on a single overseas supplier for key raw materials. This caused stock shortages, increased freight costs, and production risks.
Action:I initiated a supplier diversification project focused on localisation. I conducted market benchmarking, identified potential local suppliers, and coordinated with R&D to test alternative materials. To overcome resistance from production teams, I arranged pilot trials to ensure quality and performance standards were maintained.
Result:Reduced supply dependency by 60% through onboarding new domestic suppliers. Cut lead times by 30% and reduced logistics costs by 15%. Strengthened ESG performance by prioritising local sourcing.

Challenge:Procure-to-Pay (P2P) processes varied across Southeast Asia and Oceania, leading to inefficiencies and inconsistent reporting. Manual tracking of requests delayed approvals and made performance analysis difficult.
Action:I led the standardisation of procurement workflows by mapping regional variations and designing a unified process within SAP. Additionally, I trained a 4-member procurement team on the new system to ensure a smooth transition.
